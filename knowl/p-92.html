In mathematics and the sciences we sometimes introduce a parameter (or new input variable), e.g. \(t\text{,}\) and write our \(x\) and \(y\) coordinates in terms of that new parameter. This is common for curves that aren’t truly functions of \(x\text{,}\) like circles or ellipses. In mathematics these are often written as a set of two parametric equations or a vector-valued function.
